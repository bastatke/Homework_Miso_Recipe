Vegan Miso Soup Recipe

Miso makes a soup loaded with flavour that saves you the hassle of making stock.

- Yotam Ottolenghi

Miso soup is traditionally made with broth that contains bonito flakes - aged and dried fish. This vegan alternative is a wonderful warm and easy meal.

Ingredients
  1. 5 2-inch pieces dried Kombu seaweed
  2. 1 oz dried shiitake mushrooms
  3. 2 ½ tbsp soy sauce
  4. 16 oz water
  5. 6 cups dashi
  6. ⅓ cup (5-6 tbsp) white or yellow miso paste
  7. ½ cup thinly sliced scallions, divided
  8. 1 ½ cup fresh shiitake mushrooms, thinly sliced
  9. 3 cups bok choy, sliced
  10. 7 oz of medium to firm tofu, diced
Recipe
  1. Make dashi broth by combining kombu, soy sauce, dried mushrooms, and water in a stock pot.
  2. Heat water to a gentle simmer, then remove from heat, cover with a lid and let sit for 30 minutes.
  3. In a small bowl or pot, whisk together miso paste and ½ cup of warmed dashi broth. Set aside for now.
  4. Add 5 ½ cups of dashi to pot over medium heat.
  5. Once the broth starts to simmer, reduce heat slightly and stir in tamari, green onion, mushrooms and bok choy and cook for 10-15 minutes.
  6. Remove soup from heat and stir in miso/broth mixture.
  7. Add tofu to a soup bowl and ladle broth over top. Add more scallions on top.

Enjoy!
